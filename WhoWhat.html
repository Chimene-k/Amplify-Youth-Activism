Here, we will learn about existing activist organizations, what they do, and how we can get involved. Remember: there are many other ways to get involved in activism that are not on this page. You can research other organizations or start your own in your community. You can reach out to them to get involved by clicking the link to get more info! Any quotes below are taken from the websites of these organizations.

ACLU Take Action Page

"Today, years of hard-fought civil liberty protections are under threat — and to influence lawmakers, we need everyone to get involved. Here’s what you can do." Check them out!

ACLU Youth Activist Scholarship Program

Opportunity for high school seniors to get involved with the ACLU! If applications are closed, you can follow the link on their page to find opportunities at an ACLU branch near you. Check them out!

Bronx Connect

"Since 1999, BronxConnect has served 2,000+ youth and young adults in its alternative programs for court-involved high-risk youth. For nearly two decades, it has been the only Bronx-based, faith-based and community-based Alternative-to-Incarceration (ATI) and Alternative-to-Detention (ATD) program serving our community." Check them out!

Changing Womxn Collective

"seeks to empower BIPOC womxn, femmes, and non-binary people’s narratives, connect young leaders, and inspire the next generation of artists" Check them out!

Gen Z Girl Gang

"GenZ Girl Gang is an inclusive community of womxn and femmes lifting each other up. Our seasonal campaigns include community events, mentorship opportunities, and informational digital content." Check them out!

Integrate NYC

Youth led organization standing for equity and integration in NYC schools Check them out!

NYCLU Teen Activist Project (TAP)

"TAP is an exciting leadership development program that engages New York City high school students as organizers and activists on civil rights and civil liberties." Check them out!

NYC Youth Civics Initiative (NYCYCI)

"We are a group of youth activists and 20+ diverse civic and advocacy organizations dedicated to promoting opportunities for sustained civic engagement." Check them out!

SustainUS

Youth led organization training and empowering young leaders to be "agents of change" and advocae for justice and sustainability Check them out!

TREEage

"Youth organization dedicated to implementing climate justice legislation in New York City and State" Check them out!

Urban Youth Collaborative

Led by students, the Urban Youth Collaborative (UYC) brings together New York City students to fight for real education reform that puts students first. Demanding a high quality education for all students, our young people struggle for social, economic, and racial justice in our schools and communities. Check them out!

Youth Over Guns

BIPOC youth led organization working to build youth power in marginalized communities throughout the nation and encourage legislative advocacy to prevent gun violence, while shifting public discourse towards an evidentiary approach to keeping schools and communities safe Check them out!

Zero Hour

"Zero Hour is a youth-led movement creating entry points, training, and resources for new young activists and organizers (and adults who support our vision) wanting to take concrete action around climate change." Check them out!
